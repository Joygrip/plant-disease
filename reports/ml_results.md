## Experimental Setup

### Dataset
- Source: `vipoooool/new-plant-diseases-dataset` (Kaggle)
- 38 classes (species × disease combinations)
- Train: ~70,000 images · Val: 8,777 · Test: 8,795
- Split: `valid/` split 50/50 stratified by class (seed=42) into val + test

### Preprocessing
- Resize to 224×224 px
- Normalize with ImageNet stats:
  - mean = (0.485, 0.456, 0.406)
  - std  = (0.229, 0.224, 0.225)

### Augmentation (train only)
- `RandomHorizontalFlip()`
- `RandomRotation(15°)`
- `ColorJitter(brightness=0.1, contrast=0.1, saturation=0.1)`

### Framework
- PyTorch · torchvision · scikit-learn
- Mixed precision training (`torch.amp`)
- Optimizer: AdamW · Schedule: cosine LR · Early stopping (patience=5)

## Limitations

- **Lab-condition dataset**: all images are from controlled conditions. Real-world field photos have background clutter, variable lighting, and partial occlusion. Expect a generalization gap.

- **Cross-species confusions**: 3 of the top-10 confused pairs involve different plant species — these should be rare and indicate the model still makes gross errors in some cases.

- **Overconfident errors**: 35.7% of wrong predictions have softmax confidence > 0.9. These are the most dangerous failures for an end-user application — the model is wrong but appears certain.

- **No temporal or geographic diversity**: the dataset does not cover seasonal variation or regional disease strains.
